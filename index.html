The Artist

About Ezhilarasi

Born in Karaikudi, Tamil Nadu, Ezhilarasi Murugesan is a Carnatic vocalist and music educator trained for over a decade under the guidance of Guru Smt. Sangeetha Sivakumar. A graduate of the Kalakshetra Foundation and the University of Madras, she continues her scholarly engagement with the tradition as a Research Scholar in Indian Music at Vels Institute of Science, Technology & Advanced Studies.

Her concerts have carried her to some of Tamil Nadu's most storied venues — from the temple precincts of Pillayarpatti and the Rama Navami Mandapam at Karaikudi, to the samadhi of Muthuswami Dikshitar and the December music season at the Vels Margazhi Utsav. Alongside her solo vocal concerts, she is a sought-after vocalist for Bharatanatyam dance programmes, lending her voice to kutcheris across Tamil Nadu, including the Natyanjali Festival at the Nataraja Temple, Chidambaram.

New Horizons

Beyond the Concert Stage

Ezhilarasi's musical curiosity reaches beyond the traditional concert circuit. An independent artist and songwriter who plays both violin and guitar, she performs original light music compositions with her own band, On The Streets of Chennai — and is actively looking to bring that same voice, and her pen, to Indian cinema.

Playback Singing

Singing for Films

With a classically grounded, versatile voice — and active experience as an independent artist performing original compositions on violin, guitar, and vocals with her own band, On The Streets of Chennai — Ezhilarasi is keen to lend her singing to film soundtracks, open to playback opportunities across Tamil and other Indian language cinema.
